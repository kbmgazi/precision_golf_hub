Golf Tips and Guides

Free advice from our PGA-certified coaches. New tips are added every week. Use the categories below to find tips for your game.

Hit it longer and straighter off the tee.

Driving

Fix Your Driver Setup in 5 minutes

Small changes to ball position and tee height can add 20 or more yards immediately. Most golfers tee the ball too low and play it too far back in their stance.

Quick tips:

  • Tee the ball so half the ball sits above the top of the club face.
  • Play the ball just inside your front heel.
  • Tilt your spine slightly away from the target at address.
Driving

Choosing the Right Driver Shaft

The wrong shaft flex is one of the most common equipment problems. Playing a shaft that is too stiff or too flexible costs you distance and accuracy.

Simple guide:

  • Swing speed under 85 mph: use Regular (R) flex.
  • Swing speed 85 to 95 mph: use Stiff (S) flex.
  • Swing speed over 95 mph: use Extra Stiff (X) flex.
  • Book a fitting to get an exact measurement.

Strike your irons cleaner and hit more greens.

Iron Play

How to Strike Your Irons Cleanly

The key to clean iron contact is to hit the ball first and then the ground. Most amateurs do the opposite - they hit the ground behind the ball.

Quick tips:

  • At address, put 60% of your weight on your front foot.
  • Play the ball in the centre of your stance for mid-irons.
  • Keep your hands slightly ahead of the ball at impact.
Iron Play

Know Your Distances

Most amateurs do not know exactly how far they hit each club. This leads to poor club selection and missed greens on the wrong side.

How to measure your distance:

  • Go to the range with a range finder or GPS watch.
  • Hit 10 shots with each iron and note the carry distance of your 7 or 8 best.
  • Write down your average - this is your real yardage, not your best ever shot.

Save shots around the green. This is where scores are made.

Short Game

The Bump and Run Chip

The safest chip shot in golf - a low, running chip using a 7 or 8 iron. It removes the risk of thinning a lob shot and is much more consistent.

How to play it:

  • Use a 7 or 8 iron and play the ball back in your stance.
  • Put most of your weight on your front foot.
  • Use your putting stroke - no wrist action at all.
  • Aim to land the ball just onto the green and let it run to the hole.
Short Game

Escaping the Bunkers Every Time

Most golfers are scared of bunkers. They do not need to be. Follow these three steps and you will get our first time , every time.

Three key steps:

  • Open your club face before you grip the club.
  • Aim your feet and body to the left of the target.
  • Hit the send about 5 cm behind the ball and follow through fully.

The fastest way to lower your score. One stroke saved on the green counts the same as a 300-yard drive.

Putting

The Gate Drill for consistent Putting

This is one of most popular drills used by Tour professionals. It forces your putter face to be square at impact every time.

How to do it:

  • Push two tees into the green just wider than your putter head, about 30 cm in front of the ball.
  • Try to roll the ball through the two tees without touching either one.
  • If you hit a tee, your face is not square at impact.
  • Do this for 10 minutes before a round to groove your stroke.
Putting

How to Improve Your Pace Control

Most three putts happen not because of poor aim but because the Ball is hit way too hard or too soft. Pace control is the most important putting skill.

Practice drill:

  • Place balls at 3 metres, 6 metres, 9 metres and 12 metres from a hole.
  • Try to hole or get each one within 30 cm of the hole.
  • Focus on your backswing length - a longer backswing means a longer put.
  • Do this for 10 minutes before a round to groove your stroke.

Play smarter golf and cut your handicap without hitting the ball any better.

Course Management

Think Like a Scratch Golfer

Low-handicap golfers rarely go for risky shots. They play to their strengths , avoid trouble and let their short game save pars.

Rules to follow:

  • If there is water or out of bounds on one side, aim well away from it.
  • A layup to 100 metres is better than a risky shot that could cost you three strokes.
  • Take one more club than you think - most amateurs come up short of the green.
  • Always play to the part of the green, not the pin.

A more flexible body makes a more powerful and consistent golf swing.

Fitness

The 10-Minute Pre-Round Warm-Up

Never go straight to the first tee cold. A short warm-up routine prevents injury and helps you play your best from the very first hole.

Routine:

  • 5 minutes: swing two clubs together slowly to loosen your shoulders.
  • 2 minutes: hit 10 chips with a wedge - small swings first.
  • 3 minutes: hit 10 putts from 2 metres to get eyes and pace right.
